Breast Augmentation

What is breast augmentation?

Breast augmentation surgery is also known as breast enlargement or enhancement and can also be referred to as a “boob job”.

Are You a Suitable Candidate?

Breast augmentation might be right for you if you have sagging, asymmetrical, or elongated breasts, or are unhappy with their shape. It's also beneficial after breast reconstruction or if you need a breast lift. You should be in good health and have realistic expectations. Discuss your goals with your surgeon to create a personalized plan.

Who Should Avoid Surgery?

Avoid surgery if you feel pressured by others to change your body. Smoking or significant medical issues might also disqualify you. Be honest with your surgeon about your health and lifestyle.

Preparing for Surgery

  • Quit Smoking: Stop at least six weeks before surgery.
  • Avoid Certain Medications: Don't take aspirin, herbal supplements, or anti-inflammatory drugs beforehand.
  • Arrange for Support: Have someone drive you home and help you during recovery.
  • Plan for Recovery: Take 2 to 3 weeks off work and avoid strenuous activities.

What to Expect During Surgery

The procedure takes 1 to 2 hours under general anesthesia. The surgeon makes an incision in a discreet area to insert the implant. Your surgeon will discuss the best options for your needs.

Aftercare and Recovery

  • Post-Surgery Care: Manage discomfort with pain relief medication and wear a support bra for 4 weeks.
  • Keep the Area Dry: Avoid getting the surgical site wet for a week.
  • Follow-Up Appointments: Attend follow-ups to monitor recovery.

Staying Healthy During Recovery

Walk lightly to improve blood flow and prevent clots. Avoid heavy lifting and strenuous activities until healed.

Understanding Risks

Common side effects include swelling, bruising, and changes in nipple sensation. Serious complications are rare but can include infection and implant rupture. Discuss concerns with your surgeon during follow-ups.

By preparing thoroughly and following your surgeon's advice, you can achieve the best results from your breast augmentation.
